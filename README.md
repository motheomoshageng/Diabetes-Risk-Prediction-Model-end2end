# Diabetes-Risk-Prediction-Model-end2end

 ## 📌 Project Overview
This project aims to develop a robust classification model to predict the likelihood of an individual having diabetes (or pre-diabetes) based on key health indicators and behavioral risk factors. By leveraging the CDC's Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS) dataset, I explored the complex relationships between lifestyle choices, demographics, and chronic conditions to build a tool that could assist in early identification of at-risk populations.

The Goal: To identify the strongest predictors of diabetes and build a high-accuracy machine learning model that balances precision and recall, providing actionable insights for public health initiatives.

## 💡 Business Problem & Motivation
Diabetes is a significant and growing public health crisis. Early identification of individuals at high risk can lead to lifestyle interventions that prevent or delay the onset of the disease, reducing healthcare costs and improving quality of life.

From a data science perspective, this project simulates a real-world healthcare analytics task:

Objective: Classify individuals into diabetic, pre-diabetic, or non-diabetic categories.

Value: Provide a data-driven foundation for targeted screening programs and personalized health recommendations.
